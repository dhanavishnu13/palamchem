Palaniappan Subramania

Hi, I’m Palan

I’m a chemist working with the chemistry of fuel cells and metal-air batteries in a research center.
I’m originally from Karaikudi in the south of India, and now living in Pilsen, Czech Republic.

I’m currently a Senior Researcher in the Renewable Energy section at New Technologies Research Centre (NTC) - University of West Bohemia, where I have a range of roles providing expertise on the chemistry of electrochemical energy storage and conversion device, as the sub-group leader for the fuel cell team in Advanced Materials department. I’ve been at NTC since 2021, after a 10-year stint in academia in Europe, Israel, and South Korea.

My professional background is in chemistry and electrochemistry. I obtained an MSc degree in 2005 and PhD in 2011 from the Alagappa University, and worked as a researcher at the IEMN-FR, Ariel University-IL and KU Leuven-BE. I have worked on material development for fuel cells, supercapacitors, metal-air batteries for over 15 years, since my PhD research project in 2006. Since then I have specialised in the electrochemistry of fuel cells, electrolysers and metal-air batteries.

At NTC, I am engaged in a variety of roles related to the electrochemical energy storage and conversion technologies, from design and development to characterisation and analytics. I am leading academic research projects, primarily focused on basic next-generation material development, diagnostics and component integration with devices with an aim to translate proof-of-concept research studies to commercial products.
Among my previous academic projects, I have worked with electroanalysis, PEM fuel cell electrode materials, zinc-air batteries, plasmon induced electrochemical transformations, biosensors, supercapactiors, and conducting polymers.

I have strong interests in vedic astrology, birth chart analysis, stock market trading, and visits to various new places to enjoy the beauty of nature (riverfronts, forests and landscape).
